What is “card intelligence”?

Card intelligence is the layer of models, rules and analytics that drives every key decision: who to approve, which limit to set, what to flag as fraud and which users to retain.

  • Credit risk scoring and limit strategies
  • Real-time fraud and abuse detection
  • Portfolio analytics and stress testing
  • Marketing and offer targeting
  • Collections, churn and lifetime value models

The decision stack for card issuers

Onboarding & KYC

Identity checks, device data and first-pass credit models decide if an application continues.

Underwriting

Probability-of-default models, income estimates and policy rules set approval and pricing.

Authorisation

Real-time models score each transaction in milliseconds and decide approve / decline / challenge.

Portfolio steering

Limit changes, line management and offers keep risk in target bands as the portfolio grows.

Collections & recovery

Behavioural models predict who will self-cure, who needs outreach and which strategy to use.

Profitability & LTV

Intelligence combines risk, revenue and cost to model long-term value per segment or user.

Key model types in card intelligence

Model type Used for Typical signals
Credit scoring Approvals & limits Income, bureau data, history, utilisation
Fraud / anomaly Blocking bad transactions Device, location, merchant, velocity, patterns
Propensity & offer Upgrades, cross-sell Spend categories, product mix, engagement
Churn & retention Save actions Activity decline, complaints, competitive signals
Collections Contact strategy Payment history, balance trends, macro indices

Governance, fairness & regulation

Intelligence for cards is heavily regulated and must be explainable, auditable and fair:

  • Clear policies for data sources and feature usage
  • Documentation of model purpose and limitations
  • Monitoring for drift, bias and stability over time
  • Human override for critical customer decisions
  • Compliance with local credit and AML rules
